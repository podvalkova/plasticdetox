Supplements

Your fish oil softgel. Your chocolate protein powder. Your slow release magnesium. The supplement industry tests for heavy metals and pesticides, but microplastics are a near total testing gap. No third party certifier (ConsumerLab, Labdoor, NSF, Informed Sport, Mamavation) tests supplements for microplastics.

I went through the published research. A 2024 Korean study found animal based omega 3 capsule oil contained about 10.6 microplastic particles per gram, and that encapsulation amplified contamination 3 to 5 times over raw oil. A 2024 Australian study found microplastics in 100% of fiber supplements tested.

This guide ranks every supplement form from cleanest to dirtiest, names the cleanest picks for vitamin D, magnesium, omega 3, creatine, and protein powder, and tells you which forms to skip entirely.
