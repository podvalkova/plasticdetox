Quick answers

Common parenting questions on lower-tox living, with deep-dive guides for each.

Are silicone bottles safer than plastic?
Yes, especially for hot liquids. Food-grade platinum-cure silicone (look for that exact phrase on the label) is heat-stable and doesn't shed particles at typical feeding temperatures. Avoid generic "silicone" without the platinum-cure designation, and never microwave any silicone product. Glass remains the gold standard.
Read the full guide →
Should I throw out my plastic baby bottles?
Not necessarily. Most microplastic exposure comes from heat. Three changes cut shedding by ~80% per studies: stop sterilizing in plastic, never warm formula in plastic, and let water cool before pouring into the bottle. Replace with glass or stainless steel as you go, no need to replace all at once.
Read the full guide →
Are GreenGuard Gold and OEKO-TEX trustworthy?
Both are legitimate but test for different things. GreenGuard Gold tests for VOC emissions (off-gassing). OEKO-TEX Standard 100 tests for chemical residues in textiles. Neither tests for everything. For comprehensive safety, also look for GOTS (organic textiles) and Made Safe certifications.
Read the full guide →
Are essential oils safe for babies?
No, not for babies under 2. Eucalyptus, peppermint, rosemary, and camphor cause respiratory distress and have been linked to seizures in young children. Lavender and chamomile in extreme dilution are safer but still avoidable. Use unscented, fragrance-free products for babies and toddlers.
Read the full guide →
What about plastic toys?
Older plastic toys (pre-2008) and imported toys without ASTM F963 or EN 71 certification carry the highest risk for BPA, phthalates, and lead. Newer US/EU-certified toys are significantly safer. For new purchases, choose untreated wood, food-grade silicone, or natural rubber whenever possible.
Read the full guide →
What water should I mix formula with?
Filtered tap water (not bottled, which is higher in nanoplastics). Use a filter rated for fluoride and PFAS removal: reverse osmosis is best. Heat the water first, then pour into the formula bottle once it's cooled to body temperature. Never warm prepared formula in a plastic bottle.
