Grocery Guide

How to Repack Everything
When You Get Home

Meat and fish
→
Mason jars (32 oz) or parchment paper
Leave headspace for freezing
Cheese
→
Bee's Wrap or Formaticum paper
Unwrap from plastic immediately
Milk
→
Glass pitcher
Pour as soon as you unpack
Yogurt & sour cream
→
Mason jars or glass bowls
Buy large tubs, portion to glass
Butter
→
Glass butter dish
Foil wrapped is lower risk
Berries & soft fruit
→
Glass container or bowl
Rinse first to remove particles
Leafy greens
→
Glass container or salad spinner
Rinse even if "pre washed"
Condiments
→
Buy in glass (Primal Kitchen, Bragg)
Transfer squeeze bottles to jars
Bread
→
Linen bread bag or bread box
Buy bakery paper bags
Protein powder
→
Half gallon Mason jar (64 oz)
Transfer from tub on day one
The goal is not perfection. Just getting food out of plastic within a few hours of arriving home eliminates the majority of ongoing exposure.
Unpack groceries into glass, steel, or paper. plasticdetox.org
